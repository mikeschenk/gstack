## Context

AI-generated expert content service for B2B brands. 3 clients × €800/month = €2,400 MRR.
50x margin (€0.40 to produce, €800 to client). Target: €100K/month (125 clients).
Immediate focus: close 2 warm prospects (SEO consultancy, electrical cables).

## Baseline Review — Critical Gaps

### CRITICAL GAP 1: No QA process before delivery
Articles are delivered without a review step. An LLM hallucinating a client's product
name, price, or claim is a trust-killing event. One bad article published = at-risk client.

### CRITICAL GAP 2: No SEO performance data
The open question in the original plan ("what happens to rankings for furniture client?")
is not a curiosity — it is the retention proof. Without it, you cannot answer "does it work?"
at a sales call or a renewal conversation. Pull Google Search Console data before any
new prospect meeting.

### CRITICAL GAP 3: Onboarding bottleneck has no implementation plan
The plan correctly names 5h onboarding as the constraint to scale, then defers it
entirely. At 10+ clients this becomes a wall. Target <1h non-interview onboarding.

## Warnings

- No contract minimum terms. Month-to-month at 125 clients = churn cliff.

- Business analysis methodology lives in your head. Not documentable = not hireable.
- LLM cost at scale: 5,000 articles/month at 125 clients ≈ €2,000/month. Margin holds; worth tracking.

## Accepted Scope

- **QA checklist before delivery** — 5-point checklist applied to every article batch before it leaves your hands:
  1. No hallucinated facts about the client's products, prices, or claims
  2. No generic AI filler ("In today's fast-paced world...")
  3. Correct product/brand names throughout
  4. Matches client voice from the business analysis interview
  5. Target keyword used naturally (not stuffed)

  This is a 30-minute document. It is the difference between "we review everything before delivery" and hoping the LLM got it right.
